# Auri — Advisor Intake Sheet / Data Collection Checklist

> **Privacy note:** All data you enter into Auri is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to any server or third party. Nothing leaves your computer.

Use this checklist to gather the information you need before setting up Auri. Having these numbers ready will let you complete the setup in one sitting.

---

## 1. Personal Details (Primary)

- [ ] **Full name** (used for display only)
- [ ] **Current age** (in years)
- [ ] **Province of residence** — determines marginal tax rates
  - Options: BC, AB, ON, QC, SK, MB, NS, NB, PE, NL
- [ ] **Risk tolerance**
  - Options: Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive

---

## 2. Spouse / Partner (if applicable)

- [ ] **Spouse full name**
- [ ] **Spouse current age**
- [ ] **Spouse province of residence** (if different)
- [ ] **Spouse risk tolerance**

---

## 3. Employment Income

**Primary:**
- [ ] **Gross annual income** — T4 Box 14 (employment income before deductions)
- [ ] **Planned retirement age**

**Spouse (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse gross annual income**
- [ ] **Spouse planned retirement age**

---

## 4. CPP Benefit

You can provide CPP in one of two ways:

**Option A — Age-65 estimate (recommended):**
- [ ] **Estimated monthly CPP at age 65**
  - Source: Log in to My Service Canada at [canada.ca/my-service-canada](https://www.canada.ca/en/employment-social-development/services/my-account.html) → "Canada Pension Plan" → view your Statement of Contributions

**Option B — Actual benefit at your planned start age:**
- [ ] **Monthly CPP benefit at your planned start age**

**Both options:**
- [ ] **Planned CPP start age** (60–70)
  - Starting before 65 reduces the benefit by **0.6% per month** (up to –36% at age 60)
  - Deferring past 65 increases the benefit by **0.7% per month** (up to +42% at age 70)

**Spouse CPP (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse monthly CPP at 65** (or at planned start age)
- [ ] **Spouse planned CPP start age**

---

## 5. OAS Benefit

- [ ] **Monthly OAS at age 65** — the 2026 maximum is approximately **$727/month** for those with 40+ years of Canadian residency after age 18. Use this default if you are unsure.
- [ ] **Planned OAS start age** (65–70)
  - Deferring each month past 65 increases OAS by **0.6% per month** (up to +36% at age 70)

**Spouse OAS (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse monthly OAS at 65**
- [ ] **Spouse planned OAS start age**

---

## 6. Employer Pension (Defined Benefit only)

If you or your spouse have a DB pension from an employer or government plan:

- [ ] **Monthly pension amount** (in today's dollars)
- [ ] **Pension start age**
- [ ] **Is it indexed to inflation?** (yes/no)

**Spouse DB pension (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse monthly pension amount**
- [ ] **Spouse pension start age**

---

## 7. Account Balances

Current market value of each registered and non-registered account. Check your most recent brokerage or bank statement.

**Primary:**
- [ ] **RRSP / RRIF balance** (combined if both exist)
- [ ] **TFSA balance**
- [ ] **Non-registered investment account balance**

**Spouse (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse RRSP / RRIF balance**
- [ ] **Spouse TFSA balance**

> **Tip:** If you upload a TD WebBroker CSV file in the Wizard, Auri will pre-fill these balances automatically.

---

## 8. Contribution Room

The amount of unused room you have available to contribute this year. This is separate from your current balance.

**Primary:**
- [ ] **RRSP room remaining** — find this on your most recent Notice of Assessment (NOA) from CRA, or log in to CRA My Account at [canada.ca/my-cra-account](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/e-services/digital-services-individuals/account-individuals.html)
- [ ] **TFSA room remaining** — also on CRA My Account under "TFSA room"

**Spouse (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse RRSP room remaining**
- [ ] **Spouse TFSA room remaining**

---

## 9. Retirement Spending

- [ ] **Annual household spending target** — the total amount you want to spend per year in retirement, in today's dollars (before inflation)
  - Include housing costs, food, travel, insurance, healthcare, hobbies, etc.
  - Exclude income taxes — Auri projects those separately

- [ ] **Large planned expenses** — one-time or irregular spending you anticipate in retirement. For each item:
  - [ ] Description (e.g., "Home renovation", "Vehicle replacement", "Europe trip")
  - [ ] **Year** (calendar year, e.g., 2032)
  - [ ] **Amount** ($)

---

## 10. Bridge Income (Part-Time / Contract Work)

If you or your spouse plan to do any paid work after your main retirement date:

- [ ] **Annual amount from part-time or contract work** ($)
- [ ] **Until what age** will this income continue?

**Spouse (if applicable):**
- [ ] **Spouse bridge income amount** ($)
- [ ] **Until what age**

---

## Quick Reference — Where to Find Each Number

| Data Point | Where to Find It |
|---|---|
| Gross income | T4 Box 14, or your most recent pay stub annualized |
| CPP estimate | My Service Canada account → Statement of Contributions |
| RRSP room | CRA My Account or most recent NOA |
| TFSA room | CRA My Account → TFSA |
| Account balances | Brokerage / bank statement (or upload your CSV) |
| OAS amount | [canada.ca/en/services/benefits/publicpensions/cpp/old-age-security](https://www.canada.ca/en/services/benefits/publicpensions/cpp/old-age-security.html) |
| DB pension amount | Your employer pension statement or HR department |
